## SCENE 3 — Four AI Models (0:30–1:15)

**[Show the multi-model merge diagram — Claude, Grok, Gemini, Social flowing into Composite Score]**

> NewLeaf uses four specialised AI engines, each covering a different corner of the information landscape.

**[Highlight Claude card]**

> First — Claude, by Anthropic. Already active in our pipeline. Claude performs real-time web searches across financial news, analyst commentary, and earnings transcripts. It synthesises multiple sources and gives us a structured sentiment assessment — with reasoning.

**[Highlight Grok card]**

> Second — Grok, by xAI. With native access to the X platform — formerly Twitter — Grok tracks influential trader posts, measures sentiment velocity, and detects meme stock surges in real time.

**[Highlight Gemini card]**

> Third — Google Gemini. Leveraging Google's search index and news aggregation, Gemini identifies forming narratives across traditional media — sector rotations, regulatory impacts, and supply chain disruptions.

**[Highlight Social Aggregator card]**

> And fourth — our Social Aggregator. This pulls from Reddit's WallStreetBets and options communities, StockTwits, and financial forums — giving us a direct read on retail sentiment.

---

## SCENE 4 — Composite Score (1:15–1:40)

**[Show the sentiment scoring formula and the three zones: Bullish, Neutral, Bearish]**

> Each AI model produces its own signal. We normalise and weight them into a single composite sentiment score from zero to one hundred.

> Claude carries thirty percent weight. Grok and Gemini each carry twenty-five. And the Social Aggregator rounds it out at twenty percent.

> A score above seventy is Bullish. Below forty is Bearish. And everything in between is Neutral — no modifier applied.

---

## SCENE 5 — Integration with Scoring (1:40–2:05)

**[Show the Integration with Opportunity Score section — Score Boost, Caution Flag, Suppress, Directional Tilt]**

> Here's the key — sentiment doesn't replace our three-pillar scoring system. It acts as a confidence modifier.

> When sentiment is strongly bullish and aligns with the technical trend, the opportunity score gets a bonus — up to plus five points.

> When sentiment is bearish but technicals say otherwise — we flag the divergence. You see it, you decide.

> And when we detect a material event — an earnings surprise, a regulatory action — that options pricing hasn't absorbed yet, we temporarily suppress that ticker from recommendations entirely. Better to miss a trade than walk into a trap.

---

## SCENE 6 — Daily Pipeline (2:05–2:25)

**[Show the Data Pipeline section with the four steps]**

> The sentiment pipeline runs on a strict daily schedule.

> Pre-market — Claude and Grok scan overnight developments. At market open — all four engines produce baseline scores. Every thirty minutes — Gemini and Social refresh. And at the close — we snapshot everything for historical comparison.

> It's systematic. It's continuous. And it never sleeps through an overnight headline.
