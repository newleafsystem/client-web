## SCENE 2 — The Score Overview (0:12–0:35)

**[Scroll to the pipeline diagram: Price Data → Options Chain → Technical → Gamma → IV → Score]**

> Every trading day, our pipeline pulls live price data from Alpaca, fetches full options chains from Alpaca and Yahoo, runs technical analysis, computes gamma walls, and evaluates implied volatility.

> All of that flows into a single score — zero to one hundred.

**[Show the three-pillar merge diagram: Gamma + IV + Trend → Opportunity Score]**

> The score is built from three independent pillars. Gamma structure — worth up to forty points. Implied volatility — up to thirty-five. And technical trend — up to twenty-five. Added together, they give you the full picture.

---

## SCENE 3 — Gamma Pillar (0:35–1:05)

**[Show the Gamma Pillar card and the cascade tier diagram]**

> The Gamma Pillar is the largest component — forty percent of the total score. It measures how well-defined the gamma walls are around a stock's current price.

> Strong put and call walls create a natural corridor where market makers defend price levels. That's exactly what premium sellers need.

**[Zoom into the cascade diagram: Tier 1, 2, 3]**

> Our scoring adapts to data availability. When full Gamma Exposure data is available — Tier One — we can score up to forty points. When only Open Interest exists — Tier Two — we cap at twenty-eight. And when neither is available, we fall back to RSI and Bollinger Bands as proxies — Tier Three — with a maximum of twenty-two.

> The system always finds a way to score. It just trusts better data more.

---

## SCENE 4 — IV Pillar (1:05–1:30)

**[Show the IV Pillar card and the three IV zones: <20%, 20-50%, >50%]**

> The IV Pillar is about finding the sweet spot. Premium selling works best when implied volatility is between twenty and fifty percent.

> Below twenty — there's simply not enough premium to collect. The risk isn't worth the reward.

> Between twenty and fifty — full marks. This is the zone where option premiums are rich enough to generate income, but the market isn't signalling danger.

> Above fifty — the score starts decaying toward zero. High IV often means something is wrong. Earnings surprises, regulatory shocks, market panics. The maths might look attractive, but the tail risk isn't priced in.

---

## SCENE 5 — Trend Pillar (1:30–1:50)

**[Show the Trend Pillar card and the formula]**

> The Trend Pillar is the smallest at twenty-five points, but it plays a crucial role. It rewards conviction — in either direction.

> A strongly bullish stock scores high. A strongly bearish stock also scores high. What scores low? A stock going nowhere. Because when there's no clear trend, we can't place strikes with confidence, and we can't select the right strategy.

> The formula centres on the trend engine's output — a zero-to-one score where both extremes produce the highest pillar values.

---

## SCENE 6 — Score Example & Bands (1:50–2:15)

**[Show the AAPL score bar example: Gamma 32, IV 28, Trend 18 = 78]**

> Let me show you a real example. Apple — scored at seventy-eight out of a hundred. Gamma at thirty-two out of forty — strong walls. IV at twenty-eight out of thirty-five — solidly in the sweet spot. Trend at eighteen out of twenty-five — moderate but clear direction.

> Seventy-eight puts Apple in the Excellent band.

**[Show the four score bands: Excellent 75-100, Good 60-74, Marginal 40-59, Avoid 0-39]**

> Seventy-five and above is Excellent — high confidence setups. Sixty to seventy-four is Good — solid with a minor weakness. Forty to fifty-nine is Marginal — trade with caution. And below forty is Avoid — multiple pillars failing, not enough edge.
